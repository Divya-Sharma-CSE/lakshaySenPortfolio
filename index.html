Career Timeline

Lakshya Sen’s career is a classic "prodigy-to-powerhouse" story.

2009–2014

The Prodigy Begins

At just 9 years old, Lakshya joined the Prakash Padukone Badminton Academy in Bengaluru. By 2014, he had already secured his first international title at the Swiss Junior International.

2016–2017

Junior World No. 1

In 2016, at age 15, he became the youngest player to reach the finals of the Senior National Championship. In February 2017, he officially attained the BWF World Junior No. 1 ranking.

2018

Global Breakthrough

A golden year where he became the Asian Junior Champion (the first Indian to do so in 53 years). He capped off his junior career with a Silver at the Youth Olympics and a Bronze at the World Junior Championships.

2019

Senior Transition

Lakshya made a seamless leap to the senior circuit, winning five titles in a single year, including his first BWF World Tour titles at the Dutch Open and the SaarLorLux Open.

2021

World Championship Medal

He announced his arrival at the elite level by winning a Bronze at the BWF World Championships, becoming one of the youngest Indians to ever medal at the event.

2022

Year of Glory

  • Won the India Open (Super 500).
  • Reached the final of the prestigious All England Open.
  • Led India to a historic first-ever Thomas Cup gold.
  • Won Gold at the Commonwealth Games.

2024

Olympic History

At the Paris 2024 Olympics, he became the first Indian male shuttler to reach an Olympic semi-final, ultimately finishing in a hard-fought 4th place.

2025

Continued Dominance

Lakshya remains a top contender, recently clinching the 2025 Australian Open title and reaching the finals of the Hong Kong Open.
